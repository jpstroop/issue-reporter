Open
Comments:
Max Kadel at 2022-09-09 16:29:35 :

My understanding is that the plan is to implement this and get sign-off from Esmé and cataloging with it on staging.

mzelesky at 2022-09-09 16:37:43 :

Is it a possibility to treat it like subjects are treated, meaning you can search for a title on each part of the uniform title?

Meaning, for example:

240 _1 $a Sonatas, $m horns (2), $n no. 4-6

You could search Sonatas Sonatas, horns (2) Sonatas, horns (2), no. 4-6

Darwin Scott is a major stakeholder with uniform titles, as they are used extensively in music scores for allowing an efficient browse.

Max Kadel at 2022-09-09 17:19:39 :

Wait, that led me to a record that does have a Uniform title displayed - https://catalog.princeton.edu/catalog/SCSB-3263617. It looks like it's coming from name_uniform_title_1display, which other records that do have a uniform_title_s do not have. Clearly more investigation is required.

mzelesky at 2022-09-09 17:22:15 :

Uniform titles exist in 2 fields.

130 is used when there is no author. 240 is used when there is a 1xx field for an author.

That SCSB record has a 240 field, while the Bible record has a 130. My hunch is the MARC fields are indexed into 2 different fields.

Max Kadel at 2022-09-09 17:40:53 :

Yes, looking at Bibdata, it looks like that's what's happening. Now the question is - should we change the indexing of name_uniform_title_1display in Bibdata, or change the display of Uniform title in Orangelight? I'm leaning towards the Bibdata side now...

Max Kadel at 2022-09-09 17:42:15 :

Better example of name_uniform_title_1display - https://catalog.princeton.edu/catalog/99125416143706421

mzelesky at 2022-09-09 18:23:52 :

I would think you'd want the values from 130 fields and 240 fields to be in the same indexed field, since there shouldn't be a record that has both a 130 and a 240. Catalogers would likely want to revise the records where both exist.
