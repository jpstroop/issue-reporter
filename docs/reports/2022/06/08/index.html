M Chen at 2022-06-08 15:10:58 :

(1) https://catalog-staging.princeton.edu/catalog/99105697413506421 Looks great!

(2) https://catalog-staging.princeton.edu/catalog/9946897643506421 (we wondered if not including the "Indigenous Studies" heading on this one is correct?) Aww this gets into the weed of cataloging rules. In this bib record, the subject heading of intererst is the following:

White people—Canada—Relations with Indians—History

The LCSH string in LC's database, in contrast, is White people—Relations with Indians Now LC indicates that it's not allowed to attach geographical subdivision after the string. (Why? If I knew I guess I'd be working at LC...) To get around the restriction, catalogers who wish to provide finer detail insert the geographical marker in between "White people" and "Relations with Indians," meaning such records would slip out of our left-match mechanism. My estimate is that the tagging would miss up to half of the bibs (around 200) with "Relations with Indians" in them due to the insertion. The number may be smaller if the bib contains other 650 that left-match other LC strings. See these examples I found in the bibs:

$aWhite people--$zAlabama--$xRelations with Indians $aWhite people--$zCanada--$xRelations with Indians $aBlack people--$zCaribbean Area--$xRelations with Indians. $aBlack people--$zGeorgia--$xRelations with Indians.

$aWhites--$xSources.--$y19th century--$xRelations with Indians $aAfrican Americans--$zFlorida--$zFranklin County--$xRelations with Indians. $aWhite people--$zIllinois--$zChicago--$xRelations with Indians. $aWhite people--$zOntario--$zRainy River (District)--$xRelations with Indians.

There are two options for you to consider. First, stick with left matching and live with the (small) losses.

Second, use a seperate matching mechanism. Note that there is a second sheet named Additional subfields in the vocabulary list.

ǂx Indian authors ǂx Indian influences ǂx Indian interpretations ǂx Indian languages ...

The presence of these topical divisions alone is suffcient to determing the relevance of the bibs to Indigenous Studies--unlike other subdivisions we talked about at our meeting on 05/27 and which Carolyn listed on a page linked above.

Would you be able to catch these subdivisions alone? Left-match from the start of the 650 field doesn't apply here, but the subfield x in bibs does need to match the terms in their entirety. For example, the first one is "ǂx Indian authors". If a bib contains "ǂx East Indian authors" in 650, then it is not a match.

(3) https://catalog-staging.princeton.edu/catalog/997737533506421 (did not previously have heading, now does) Looks great! Thanks!
