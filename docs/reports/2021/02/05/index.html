#233 How should DataSpace handle the versioning of data sets?

Creator:
James R. Griffin III
Created:
2020-11-17 17:08:29
Status:
Closed
Comments:
Kevin McElwee at 2021-02-05 17:51:28 :

Proposals to address this problem are being tracked in this Google doc: https://docs.google.com/document/d/13ibT_OhCpgyW66asKcn_cASqQkJ8ArGv8qV6vnq0Q50

Kevin McElwee at 2021-02-05 17:51:35 :

Conclusion

There remains no industry standard for versioning large datasets. In 2015 the Research Data Alliance interviewed 33 organizations including NASA and Zenodo, and found 33 different workflows. They pursued some kind of standard process for 4 years and, from my reading, were still unable to delineate clear guidelines.

PRDSâ€™s goals are to enact a data versioning workflow more open and rigorous than any of our peers, wanting every version properly cited and quickly downloadable through a GUI, without sacrificing storage efficiency. But Globus and Git, the two pieces of software necessary for efficient data transfer and version storage, have not been combined anywhere.

PRDSâ€™s goals arenâ€™t unique to Princeton, giving DRDS the unique opportunity to build that infrastructure if given significant developer time and if it were made a singular priority. Combining S3 versioning, the Globus CLI and Python SDK, and Git LFS, there may be a path forward to create a user interface that points directly to the version requested, but that would require a significant amount of custom infrastructure, with no promise of success. If successful, however, weâ€™d be solving a problem central to many of our peers.

In the short term, there exists no way to efficiently store versioned items in Globus in a user-friendly way. PRDS will need to determine priorities. Assuming strict adherence to their own standards, either (a) they can store datasets inefficiently, and simply double their datasets for each version, or (b) use git to version their datasets and provide documentation allowing users to checkout earlier versions of the dataset.
