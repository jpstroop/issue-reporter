In the current workflow, we download the Getty Portal, winnow out the non-Cicognara records, and convert the remaining records to TEI documents: a TEI header with the bibliographic data + a element for the IIIF manifest. In this capacity, the TEI documents are little more than bibliographic records expressed in a well-known, well-documented, and validatable bibliographic format. Then we construct a teiCorpus for each Catalogo item, with header data coming from the Catalogo and the components coming from the Getty documents (associated via dclnum). These Item-level TEI documents are used to construct the static site and the new Fuse index.

I propose we treat these item-level components as we do the encoded Catalogo: as first-class objects, maintained under version control and curated and edited as the Catalogo is. Our staff can easily be taught to update these files to correct errors in the partner data, and new records can be appended to the Item documents when they are added to the Getty Portal (this seldom happens in practice).
