The above-described AJAX issue appears to be inconsistently occurring across browsers, and is not the root cause of the issue of not being able to see submissions in a published issue. While upgrading to OJS 3.3.0-14 simplifies the workflow, this issue is resolvable within the current UI.

Issues 16, 17, 18, and 19 were fixed (and confirmed as fixed with Yuan and Hannah over Slack) through the following method.

Each submission that is linked to a published issue must be published in production. Submissions can be unpublished after an issue has been published, and would therefore disappear from the public UI. If submissions are not appearing in a published issue, this can be fixed as follows:

Step 1

Go to the "Submissions" section and locate the submission that is not displaying. These are searchable under "All Active" or "Archives," depending on the state of the submission. If the submission's state is anything other than "Published," it will not display publicly in the issue table of contents.

Step 2

Once you have located the submission, click the "View" button.

Step 3

From the "Workflow > Production" tab, click on the "Schedule for Publication" button.

Step 4

You will be forwarded to the "Workflow > Publication" tab for the submission. Click the "Schedule for Publication" button here as well.

Step 5

If everything is correctly set within the submission, you will receive a confirmation dialog box like the following:

Click "Publish" to complete publishing the submission.

Step 6

Once a submission is published, you will see the red band warning that the submission is published and cannot be edited:

If you need to edit a submission that has been published, you will need to unpublish it first, then re-publish it when you are done.

Step 7

To validate that your submission should be visible in the public UI, you can look it up in the "Submissions > Archives" section. If your submission is present in the search results and has the solid green "Published" label as shown below, your submission should be correctly configured to display in the public UI:
