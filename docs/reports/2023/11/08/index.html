I had a hard time finding any examples of similar sorts of calls to action in search interfaces, however I also went through favicons and other different UX articles and came up with a few things we could try:

Options

  • Keep the megaphone icon, to harmonize with what is on the catalog homepage
  • Remove the icon altogether
  • Go with one of the following (screenshot and icon name from Bootstrap Icons):

flag or flag-fill

person-raised-hand

binoculars or binoculars-fill

card-list

list-columns-reverse

Some sources consulted, listing here for ease of reference:

Statements on harmful content (none of these catalogs had calls to action within items or search results that I could find): * https://hornbakelibrary.wordpress.com/2019/02/26/offensive-content-in-our-collections/ * https://biblio.csusm.edu/content/potentially-harmful-language-and-content * https://library.umkc.edu/policies/harmful-content/ (couldn't search the catalog without a UMKC account) * https://library.temple.edu/policies/scrc-statement-on-potentially-harmful-language-in-archival-description-and-cataloging * https://www.archives.gov/research/reparative-description/harmful-content
