#2040 Index FAST subject headings

Creator:
Esm√© Cowles
Created:
2022-12-05 18:35:09
Status:
Open
Comments:
mzelesky at 2023-01-30 20:12:23 :

Initial analysis: https://www.oclc.org/content/dam/oclc/fast/FAST-quick-start-guide-2022.pdf is a guide to FaST.

It lists 9 sections of FAST: Topical Personal Names Corporate Names Meetings Named Events Uniform Titles Chronological Geographic Form/Genre

Of those, Named Events is the only area without a direct equivalent in LCSH.

The rest are simply derived from the LCSH equivalents, with some key differences. 1. Place names are always hierarchical and always in their 'natural order'. a. LCSH: Fuji, Mount (Japan) b. FaST: Japan -- Mount Fuji 2. Name-title headings are formed in FaST by starting with the title, then placing the name after. a. LCSH: Vonnegut, Kurt. Slaughterhouse-five b. FaST: Slaughterhouse-five (Vonnegut, Kurt) 3. In general, FaST consists of the individual facets of assigned LCSH split into the 9 segments listed above. a. LCSH: 650 _0 $a Internal migrants $z China $z Beijing $v Fiction. b. FaST: 1. 650 _7 $a Internal migrants. 2. 651 _7 $a China $z Beijing. 3. 655 _7 $a Fiction.

There are other differences, but those are the most significant.

I am running some scripts to gather data on how FaST is currently used at PUL. 1. Find records that do not have LCSH/LCGFT but have FaST headings. 2. Of records that have LCSH/LCGFT and FaST, which records have headings unique to the FaST vocabulary that aren't directly represented by LCSH/LCGFT? This will also show all FaST headings on records with both vocabularies.

My hope is that these analyses will be used to inform us how to implement this request and its priority.

mzelesky at 2023-01-30 21:54:31 :

See this report for records with FaST headings but no LC headings: https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1N_Orxap8UTb4eJt8NDZKhnQA4wQp95ShI4fphkOf8ao/edit?usp=sharing

There are 2,635 such records out of 11,802,517 unsuppressed records in Alma. Records have not been further assessed (belonging to the Community Zone, records that are not yet fully cataloged, etc.).

mzelesky at 2023-01-30 21:59:37 :

See this report for records with LC headings that also have unique FaST headings: https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1Yt-fARTeLg_V2ok-kVhUAWUijAbnLuHEuvPQgjGhhT4/edit?usp=sharing

There are 353,931 such records out of 11,802,517 records in Alma.

Many of them have near-equivalent matches in LC, e.g., World War, 1939-1945 vs. World War (1939-1945). Many others only have chronological headings, e.g., 1900 - 1999, which were derived from other areas of the record.

mzelesky at 2023-01-30 22:02:21 :

See this report for records with LC headings that have FaST headings completely duplicated by LC portions: https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1o4wkiChs1mFGI7E_yE80sMchxGiQirYLBdONCyEsNmU/edit?usp=sharing

There are 422,873 such records out of 11,802,517 records in Alma.

mzelesky at 2023-01-30 22:08:12 :

Overall summary: 1. Roughly 7% of our records have FaST headings. 2. Roughly 54% of those records have FaST headings with information that is entirely duplicated by LC headings. 3. Roughly 0.02% of our records only have FaST headings.
